Executive Summary

The Bead Bar's codebase reflects a mature startup architecture, leveraging Expo SDK 54 and Supabase efficiently. The recent upgrade to realistic SVG renders for core products significantly elevates the brand aesthetic from "generic shop" to "bespoke boutique." The primary risks involve logic duplication across environments and high-friction authentication barriers that may hinder conversion for the target Gen Z demographic.

Screen-by-Screen Breakdown

1. Authentication & Onboarding

  • UX Friction The Double Wall: Requiring a Phone Number + 6-digit PIN + SMS OTP creates a very high barrier to entry. Users expect "Magic Link" simplicity. Recommend removing the mandatory PIN for first-time sign-ups to reduce drop-off.
  • Logic Risk Captcha Timeout: If a user is on a throttled connection, the WebView-based Captcha might hang. Ensure the "Verify" button has a timeout state and a clear "Try Again" path.
  • Feature Gap Social Proof: Missing a "Guest Checkout" or "Browse before Login" option. Forcing login to see the builder is a major bounce-rate risk.

2. The Bracelet Builder

  • UX Win Tactile Realism: The new matte sage and gold luster SVGs provide excellent visual feedback. This "What You See Is What You Get" (WYSIWYG) approach reduces customer returns.
  • Data Integrity State Persistence: Currently, design progress is ephemeral. If the app backgrounding results in a memory purge, the design is lost. Implement local AsyncStorage caching for builder drafts.
  • Friction Reset Panic: Changing the "Base Style" currently wipes the pattern selection. Add a confirmation modal: "Changing your base will clear your current pattern. Continue?"

3. Cart & Fulfillment

  • Feature Gap BFF Synergy: Since the brand emphasizes friendship, the cart should suggest "Add a matching bracelet for $10" when one is added.
  • Logic Risk Inventory Race: The checkout flow relies on client-side state for pricing. While the Edge Function re-verifies this, ensure stock is "reserved" for 5 minutes during the Stripe session to prevent overselling of limited drops.

Critical Issues

CRITICAL: Single Source of Truth Logic Pricing and compatibility matrices are duplicated across Web, App, and Edge Functions. This is a "time bomb" for maintenance. If the price of gold charms increases, you must edit three files in sync or face checkout errors.
MEDIUM: Offline Mode Logic The app lacks a robust "Offline" banner. Since Supabase auth and builder assets require network, the app fails silently or shows generic errors when the user enters a tunnel or low-signal area.
MEDIUM: RLS Policy Completeness While profiles are protected, ensure all future orders and wishlists tables have auth.uid() = user_id policies to prevent "ID enumeration" attacks where users could potentially guess order IDs of others.

The "Delight" Factor

3 Ways to Move from Functional to Exceptional:

1. Haptic Feedback (Physicality): Use expo-haptics to trigger a Selection impact when a user taps a bead and a Success vibration when the bracelet loop is completed. It makes the digital product feel "held."

2. AR Wrist Try-On: Integrate a simple AR overlay. Even a non-tracking static "overlay your wrist here" photo mode would allow users to see the scale of the bracelet relative to their hand.

3. Collaborative Design: A "Send to Bestie" button that shares a unique URL. When opened, it loads the builder with the first bracelet pre-set, inviting the friend to design the second half of the set.
